Three maps are presented: `area_currents` (818 of 2948), `node_detail` (838 of 2948) and `region_overview` (839 of 2948).

### `area_currents` (818 of 2948)

- Shows a map of Binbridge Isle with the Elen and Belinus currents.
- The entry point of the Belinus current is defined as **Bembridge Down** (Head of the IOW Serpent).
- The Belinus current passes through the node Yaverland.
- The entry point of the Elen current is the **Nostril caves at Culver Down**.
- The Elen current passes through the nodes Bembridge Fort and Centurion's Copse.
- The node Bembridge Windmill is an auxiliary, and is not a node of any currents.

### `node_detail` (838 of 2948)

- Shows the black central line on the left of the map. The black central line passes near Bloodstone Spring to Ashey.
- Two currents meet at the node Devil's Punchbowl.
- Purple Elen current passes through Bloodstone Spring (attributed to Eaglehead and Bloodstone Copses; central line passes within 250 m and captures springs and woodland in the area), continuing between Brading Down and Nunwell House, then goes through the node of focus Devil's Punchbowl, and then finishes west to east to Brading Church.
- Gold Belinus current starts from the bottom map at Brading Roman Villa, Adgestone Vineyard, then the node of focus Devil's Punchbowl, then carries on north east without passing through a particular landmark, but its final direction takes us to Ryde.

### `region_overview` (839 of 2948)

A much richer map showing many more placemarkers.

**Central line (black):** Passes through Battery Gardens and two distinct churches: Christ Church (within 50 m) and St Patrick's Church (within 70 m). They are two neighbour churches in the same area within 100 metres.

*Note: Originally considered Battery Gardens as anchor; revised to Widford after canonical-bearing fit analysis (see Section 2.4 of the protocol).*

The black central line passes near:
- Bloodstone Spring (as seen in `node_detail`)
- East Ashey Earthworks (Medieval settlement 100m south east and 350m north east of East Ashey Manor Farm; exact location presented on Historic England website; line passes within 25 m)
- Kemphill (Farm within 200 m)
- Upton (Upton Cross within 500 m; Upton is the name on the ebook map)

The black central line carries on north-west without passing through any near close-tight landmark, then beyond the coast. Most nodes are near enough (within our 250 m threshold) but not exactly on the black central line, except the southernmost point (the circular plant bed).

Eleanor's Grove (a section of the A3054 road between Fishbourne Lane and Quarr Abbey) is also present; central line passes within 1 km; near Elen current.

**Elen current (purple):** Enters at **Culver Down (The Nostrils caves)** , then passes through:
- Bembridge Fort
- Centurion's Copse (Wolverton, site of)
- Brading
- Devil's Punchbowl (meeting point of the two currents)
- Nunwell House
- Bloodstone Spring
- East Ashey Earthworks
- Ashey
- Ruin (Quarr Abbey remains; ancient 12th-century medieval Cistercian monastery)
- Quarr Abbey (main recent building; 20th-century Benedictine monastery)
- then carries on beyond the coast

**Belinus current (gold):** Enters at **Bembridge Down (Head of the IOW Serpent)** , then passes through:
- Yaverland
- Brading Roman Fort
- Devil's Punchbowl (meeting point of the two currents)
- Swanmore
- Ryde
- Binstead
- then carries on beyond the coast

*Note: Red Cliff and Whitecliff Ledge appear on the map but are not the entry points; the entry points are Bembridge Down (Belinus) and Culver Down / The Nostrils (Elen). These nodes are retained as auxiliary (`is_canonical = FALSE`) for completeness.*
